Getting to Know Pinyin and Tones in Mandarin

Si, Shi, Se, She. Each of these words has a different pronunciation, coupled with the tone. ach word has a different meaning.

This makes many people feel afraid and confused about learning Mandarin. However, Mandarin is the number one most widely used language in the world, you know!

If you are thinking about starting to learn Mandarin, but where do you start? You can start by recognizing the pronunciation and tone first.

Pronunciation is the most basic part of mastering Mandarin. Without proper pronunciation, words with the same sound can have different meanings.

What is pinyin?

Mandarin is not a phonetic language. That is, the pronunciation of a word is not related to the writing of its character. The characters in Mandarin that are commonly used for writing are called Hanzi(汉字).

Pinyin is a special system created for learning the pronunciation of Mandarin characters. Usually, pinyin is also used by Chinese people to type hanzi on computers or smartphones.

Pinyin writing uses alphabets. However, the spelling and pronunciation are different. Pinyin letters are not read exactly like in English.

There are consonants that are read exactly like English, there are also those that are different. There are also some additional consonants.

Consonants in Mandarin

Consonants that are read the same as English include:

  • p, m, f, t, n, k, sh, ch

Meanwhile, the consonants whose sound is slightly different from English are:

  • h, Varies between h in English hat and ch in Scottish loch.
    example: 火 (huǒ) – pronounced 'huo' – meaning: fire
  • j, pronounced like 'ch'
    example: 叫 (jiào) – pronounced 'chiao' – meaning: shout
  • z, pronounced like 'dz'
    IPA symbol:[ts]
    example: 脏 (zàng) – pronounced 'dzang' – meaning: dirty
  • c, pronounced like 'ts'
    IPA symbol:[tsh]
    example: 从 (Cóng) – pronounced ‘tsong’ – meaning: from

There are also consonants that do not exist in English, including:

  • zh, pronounced like 'c' but with the tongue bent towards the roof of the mouth
    contoh: 这 (Zhè) – pronounced ‘ce’ – meaning: this

Some consonants have special rules, for example:

  • If 'w' is followed by the letter 'u', it is pronounced like 'woo'
    example: 午安 (Wǔ ān) – pronounced ‘woo an’ – meaning: good afternoon
  • If 'y' is followed by the letter 'i', it is pronounced like the letter 'e'
    example: 一 (Yī) – pronounced ‘e’ – meaning: one
  • If 's', 'sh', 'c', 'ch', 'z', and 'zh' are followed by the letter 'i', then the letter 'i' is pronounced like the letter 'e'. Example:
    • 四 (Sì) – pronounced ‘se’ – meaning: four
    • 是 (Shì) – pronounced ‘she’ – meaning: is
    • 吃 (Chī) – pronounced ‘che’ – meaning: eat
    • 自己 (Zìjǐ) – pronounced ‘ce ci’ – meaning: oneself

    • There is one additional vowel, namely 'ü'. The letter 'ü' is pronounced like 'ee' then slowly round your lips(like when you whistle). Example:

    • 鱼 (Yú) – pronounced ‘yee’ – meaning: fish

    Tone

    Mandarin is a tonal language. That is, the sound spoken affects the meaning of the words spoken. A word, if said in the wrong tone, can cause miscommunication.

    Mandarin has four tones. The table below can provide some explanation regarding the tones in Mandarin.

    Tone Sound Stroke Example
    Tone 1 Flat high voice ˉ 妈 (Mā)
    Tone 2 The tone rises from low to high as if you are saying a question ˊ 麻 (má)
    Tone 3 The tone curves from down to then up again, according to the stroke ˇ 马 (Mǎ)
    Tone 4 The notes fall from high to low and are pronounced short and strong ˋ 骂 (Mà)

    The third tone in Mandarin has a special rule. When a third tone is followed by another third tone, the tone of the first word automatically changes to the second tone. However, in pinyin, the second tone is still written in the form of the third tone.

    Example:
    你 + 好 (Nǐ+ hǎo) becomes 你 好 (ní hǎo)
